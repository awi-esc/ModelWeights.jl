Model weights

The weighting method that we use was developed by Knutti et al. (2017) that is based on work from Sanderson et al. (2015) (see References).

Basic computation of weights

The overall weight for model $i$ is computed based on its performance- and independenct weights, $w^P_i$ and $w^I_i$:

\[w_i = a_0 \cdot w^P_i \cdot w^I_i \]

The performance and independence weights are defined as follows:

\[w^{P}_i = a_1 \cdot e^{-(\frac{D_i}{\sigma_D})^2}\]

\[w^{I}_{i} = a_2 \cdot \frac{1}{\sum_j e^{-\left( \frac{S_{i,j}}{\sigma_S} \right)^2}} \]

\[S_{i,j}\]

refers to the generalized distances between models $i$ and $j$ and $D_i$ refers to the generalized distances between predictions of model $i$ and observatioanl/reanalysis data $\sigma_S$ and $\sigma_D$ are hyperparameters that specify the strength of the independence- and performance weighting respectively and $a_0, a_1, a_2$ are normalization constants.
